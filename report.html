Introduction

Over the last century, constructing methods and technics have become more advanced and constructing codes have become increasingly stringent, and new building materials have been developed to meet the evolving needs. It can be interesting to compare the wall materials for US commercial buildings used before and now. This report attempts to answer the following questions:

How does the choice of wall construction materials evolve over time? Are the trends different across census divisions?

To answer these questions, I first compute the proportion of wall construction material types by year of construction to analyze the overall trend. Then, I further divide the data by census division to see if different regions show distinct patterns.

Data / Methods

Data

This project uses the 2012 US Commercial Building Energy Consumption Survey (CBECS) published by the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA):

  • 2012 CBECS survey microdata (2012_public_use_data_aug2016.csv)
  • Variable and response codebook (2012microdata_codebook.xlsx)

Methods

  • Point estimates of percent of wall materials are estimated from the full sample using the full sample building weight (FINALWT), and 95% confidence intervals are constructed based on the replicate sub-samples by using the replicate weights (FINALWT1 FINALWT197).
  • As per the documentation, variances of estimations are estimated by:
\(\hat{V} (\hat{\theta}) = \sum_{r=1}^R \alpha_r(\hat{\theta}_r - \hat{\theta})^2\)
  • 95% Confidence intervals are constructed using:
\(\hat{\theta} \pm \Phi^{-1}(.975)\sqrt{\hat{V}(\hat{\theta})}\)

The data files and source code can be found in this GitHub repository.

Conclusion

  • Overall trend:
    • Although on a downward trend, brick, stone, or stucco have always been one of the most common options for commercial buildings wall material.
    • Sheet metal panels steadily gained its popularity over the last century.
    • Concrete block or poured concrete are popular among buildings constructed in the 1946 - 1969 period.
    • Glass has never been dominantly used as wall material.
